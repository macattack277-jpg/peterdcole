Rain

2007

Painted and Patinated Brass, Bronze and Aluminium

55 x 130 x 15 cms

I believe this artwork was created during my time at Langley, though it encapsulates the essence of living in Kyneton, with a pronounced church-like element, especially notable in the strong yellow cross sitting atop it. While the cross could evoke Malevich's influence, it was more inspired by rainy days, a frequent occurrence in Kyneton. The grey gloss adds a reflective quality. Many of my colours were glossy, and I often used marine paint for its durability and ease of application. The outdoor setting at Langley allowed for convenient spray painting, with the paint setting in the atmosphere, creating a smooth finish without the need for polishing.
