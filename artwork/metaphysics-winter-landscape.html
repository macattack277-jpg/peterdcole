Metaphysics of a Winter Landscape

1991

Painted and Patinated Bronze, Brass and Copper

47 x 80 x 19 cms

This piece is a reflection on the landscape, where minimalism meets with mathematical symbolism. The wooden square represents the earth, suggesting architectural structures or the grid that underlies our surroundings. The two moons, they're like a dance, changing and shifting. The copper, representing the moon, adds this glow, especially thinking back to those Langley nights with the vast sky. The sky out there was incredible, and I'd lie in bed watching comets go by. It was extraordinary, a universe playing with the balance of structure and nature.
